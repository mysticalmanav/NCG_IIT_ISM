About us

Motivation
The NCG was initially conceived on account of limited education in Geodesy at the Regional level. Although courses on Surveying and Mapping, Geoinformatics, Remote Sensing/ GIS, etc. are being taught at a few universities, none delve into the fundamentals and advanced concepts in Geodesy. Further, Regional expertise in areas such as Geodesy and SAR interferometry, is rather limited. Thus, there is a need for the development and training of human resources in the aligned areas.

Vision
To act as the hub of excellence in teaching and research in Geodesy at the Regional and interRegional level by way of preparing well-trained human resources at post-graduate level, quality publications in reputed journals and through collaborative research with interRegional institutes/institutions to contribute in the infrastructure development of the country.

Objective
Strengthen activities in the area of Geodesy Infrastructure, Education, Capacity Building, Research and Development in sync with objectives of NCG

  1. Capacity Building
    Strengthening the existing PG programme in Geomatics being offered by the Institute (16 Years)
    Offering co-branded diploma course in Geodesy with focus on Mining, Earth Sciences, and Environment
    Short and long training programmes on precise surveying tools and their applications in the niche areas of the institute.
    Faculty development programs to enhance their skills on Geodesy and modern surveying tools & techniques.
    Development of e-learning resources on Geodetic Surveying Applied to Mining and Earth Sciences.
  2. Research & Development
    To promote and undertake R&D and Industry sponsored projects on Geodetic Surveying in the fields of mining, mineral and earth sciences sectors including environment and forest.
    To develop technologies and process to solve the survey problems of special nature in the mining and earth sciences areas using advanced surveying techniques such as GNSS, LIDAR, and integrated SAR (Dump slope, Subsidence etc.)
    Development of ground based and air-borne compact radar sensors including processing tools for real-time slope and landslides monitoring.
  3. Outreach Activities
    To organize special lecture series, workshops, conferences on geodesy related theme.
    Acting as the Regional Resource Centre for extensive support (laboratory, equipment, training, library, Software, etc.) to students and researchers from various universities and institutions and advise state/central government departments on all issues related to geodesy.
    Strengthening and supporting Govt. mandated mission on Geospatial Technology in sync with organizations such as SOI, NRSC, NGRI, GSI, CMPDI etc. in establishing required infrastructure including geodetic data acquisitions, processing, and representations.
    The Centre in collaboration will recommend policies, suggest frameworks, for implementing Advanced Surveying Technologies in various important sectors of the country and their sustainable growth.
